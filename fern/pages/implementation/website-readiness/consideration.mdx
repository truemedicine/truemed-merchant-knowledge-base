Once a customer knows you accept HSA/FSA, they'll want to understand how it works, whether they qualify, and what the process looks like. This stage is about building confidence so nothing stands between awareness and purchase. The two highest-impact assets here are a dedicated landing page and an updated FAQ.

## Create an HSA/FSA Landing Page

A dedicated landing page gives you a single URL to drive paid ads, email campaigns, and social traffic to. It educates qualified shoppers on how HSA/FSA payments work for your brand and serves as the hub for all your marketing efforts.

A strong landing page answers four questions in order:

1. **What is this?** Explain that customers may be able to use pre-tax HSA/FSA funds on eligible products.
2. **Am I eligible?** Describe the brief clinical intake form and how eligibility is determined by an independent licensed practitioner.
3. **Which products qualify?** Showcase your eligible products or link to your HSA/FSA collection.
4. **How do I check out?** Walk through the Truemed checkout flow step by step.

## Update Your FAQ Page

Customers will have questions about HSA/FSA eligibility. Adding a FAQ section to your existing FAQ or help page preempts support tickets and builds trust. Start with these three questions:

### "What is HSA/FSA and can I use it here?"

Explain that HSA (Health Savings Account) and FSA (Flexible Spending Account) are pre-tax health funds that may be used to purchase eligible health products. Note that eligibility depends on individual circumstances and is determined by an independent licensed practitioner through a brief clinical intake form at checkout.

### "How do I pay with my HSA/FSA?"

Walk the customer through the process:

1. Add eligible products to your cart.
2. At checkout, select "Pay with Truemed" as the payment method.
3. Complete a brief clinical intake form (takes about 2 minutes).
4. If the practitioner determines medical necessity, a Letter of Medical Necessity (LMN) is issued and the HSA/FSA payment is processed.

### "Which products are eligible?"

Link to your HSA/FSA product collection. If you have a landing page, link to the eligible products section there. Avoid listing specific products in the FAQ itself, since your eligible product list may change over time.
