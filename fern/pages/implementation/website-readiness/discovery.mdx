Discovery is about making sure customers encounter HSA/FSA messaging within their first few clicks. Navigation links, homepage banners, and product badges all serve this purpose. A shopper who doesn't know you accept HSA/FSA can't use it, so this stage is about closing that awareness gap before they ever reach a product page.

## Add HSA/FSA to Your Site Navigation

Navigation links are permanent, persistent, and trusted. A dedicated nav item ensures customers researching HSA/FSA options find it quickly, without relying on site search or scrolling.

**Good:** A text link in the footer under "Support" or "Legal."
**Great:** A dedicated "HSA/FSA" link in your top utility navigation bar, plus a link in the footer under "Financing & Discounts."

### How to Implement

The exact steps depend on your platform, but in most cases this is a simple menu edit:

1. In your site admin, open your navigation settings.
2. Add a new menu item labeled "HSA/FSA" or "HSA/FSA Eligible."
3. Link it to your HSA/FSA landing page (if you have one) or your HSA/FSA product collection.
4. For the footer, add the same link under a section like "Financing & Discounts" or "Ways to Pay."

If you don't have a landing page yet, link to your HSA/FSA product collection for now and update it later when you build one in the [Consideration](/marketing/website-optimization/consideration) stage.

## Add a Homepage Banner

A homepage banner reaches every visitor, not just those who land on a product page. Top-performing brands keep a persistent HSA/FSA banner or rotate it into their banner carousel. A banner in the hero slot or a pinned utility bar both work well.

### What to Include

Your banner should communicate three things: that you accept HSA/FSA, that customers may save (with appropriate disclaimers), and a clear call to action. Keep the copy short. One line of text plus a CTA button is enough.

**Example copy:** "Your purchase may be HSA/FSA eligible. Learn how to save with Truemed."

Link the CTA to your HSA/FSA landing page or product collection.

## Create an HSA/FSA Product Collection with Badges

Group your eligible products into a dedicated collection and add eligibility badges to product cards. For merchants with large catalogs, add a left-rail filter option ("HSA/FSA Eligible") so customers can narrow any category. A dedicated "HSA/FSA Eligible" category page that surfaces all eligible products in one place tends to outperform badges alone.

You don't need to determine which products are eligible. Truemed determines eligibility based on IRS guidelines and provides your approved product list during onboarding.

### Product Badges

Add a small badge or tag to each eligible product card. The badge should be visible on both the collection/category page and in search results. Common formats include a small text label ("HSA/FSA Eligible"), a colored tag, or a small icon.

For merchants with Shopify, product badges can typically be added through your theme's product card template or via a badge app. Your developer can handle this in most cases.

### Collection Page

Create a dedicated collection or category page that groups all eligible products together. This gives you a single URL you can link from navigation, banners, email campaigns, and ads.

### Filter Option

For merchants with large, mixed-eligibility catalogs, a left-rail filter ("HSA/FSA Eligible") lets customers narrow any category to see only eligible products. This is more of a development task and may require your dev team.

<Warning>
Badges must be product-level. For mixed catalogs, only eligible products should carry the badge. Do not use a banner at the top of a mixed category page that implies all products are eligible. This is a blanket brand claim and is non-compliant.
</Warning>
